Glasgow Diversity & Inclusion Groups - Outreach Contacts

Community over Code Glasgow 2026 - October 11-14, Hilton Glasgow

This page lists Glasgow-area diversity-in-tech groups and organizations. We’re inviting their members to attend and participate in Community over Code Glasgow (October 11-14, 2026) and the hackathon.

Diversity-in-Tech Organizations

Scotland Women in Technology (SWiT)

  • Website: https://www.scotwomenintech.com/
  • About: Scotland-wide organization supporting women in technology. Runs annual awards, events, and networking.
  • Relevance: Established community with engaged members who’d benefit from conference exposure.

Ladies of Code (Glasgow)

  • Website: https://www.meetup.com/ladies-of-code-glasgow/
  • About: Regular meetups celebrating and supporting women in the tech industry. Platform for networking, knowledge sharing, and collaboration.
  • Relevance: Active local group, likely receptive to a hackathon invite.

Coding Girls Glasgow

  • Website: https://glasgow.coding-girls.com/
  • About: Free coding events, workshops, and mentorship for girls and women in Glasgow.
  • Relevance: Ideal audience for beginner-friendly hackathon tasks.

Ada Scotland Festival

  • Website: https://ada.scot/
  • About: Focused on gender diversity in computing and tech in Scotland. Highlights the gap (19% female in Scottish Higher computing, 4.1:1 male:female ratio in UK computing degrees).
  • Relevance: Aligned mission; potential cross-promotion partner.

Code Division

  • Website: https://www.codedivision.co.uk/
  • About: Non-profit supporting underrepresented groups with digital skills. 83% of learners are women. Programmes in Cyber, Coding, Marketing, Data, and AI. 950+ learners upskilled.
  • Relevance: Their learners would gain real-world open source experience at the hackathon.

The Young Women’s Movement - Young Women Code

  • Website: https://www.youngwomenscot.org/young-women-code
  • About: Part of the broader Young Women’s Movement in Scotland. Addresses the 23% female representation in Scotland’s digital technology workforce.

Glasgow Women in Computing Science (GWiCS)

  • Website: https://www.gla.ac.uk/schools/computing/diversity/activities/
  • About: University of Glasgow group open to all staff and students (female/non-binary/male). Focus on research-level CS and above.
  • Relevance: University-based, could bring students and researchers.

How to Use This Page

When reaching out to these groups:

  • Emphasize that Community over Code is a welcoming, inclusive environment
  • Highlight the hackathon as beginner-friendly with mentors available
  • Offer free or discounted passes where possible
  • Suggest it as a portfolio-building and networking opportunity
  • Note the “hallway greeter” volunteers who welcome newcomers
