> **[Community over Code Glasgow 2026](https://communityovercode.org/)** - October 11-14, Hilton Glasgow


A guide to bars, pubs, and nightlife near the Community over Code
Glasgow venue (Hilton Glasgow, 1 William St, city centre).

Glasgow has a legendary pub culture. Here are some highlights within
walking distance of the conference venue.

## Good Beer Guide Pubs (CAMRA-recommended)

### Babbity Bowster
- **What:** Tucked-away pub-restaurant-hotel in Merchant City with a reputation for excellent beer, wine, and food. Beer garden.
- **Where:** 16-18 Blackfriars Street, Glasgow G1 1PE ([map](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=16-18%20Blackfriars%20Street%2C%20Glasgow%20G1%201PE))
- **Good for:** Craft beer, quieter conversation, good food

## Classic Glasgow Pubs

### The Belle
- **What:** Mixed-clientele favourite with suits, students, and dogs by an open fire
- **Where:** Great Western Road, Glasgow ([map](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=The%20Belle%2C%20Great%20Western%20Road%2C%20Glasgow))
- **Good for:** Cosy atmosphere, good pints, open fire in October

### Felson's
- **What:** Pool tables, HD sports screens, burgers and pints
- **Where:** City centre, Glasgow ([map](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Felson%27s%2C%20Glasgow%20city%20centre))
- **Good for:** Groups, casual hangouts, watching football

### Hope & Vincent
- **What:** Pub/lounge bar with pool table, jukebox, and an eight-foot sports screen
- **Where:** City centre, Glasgow ([map](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Hope%20and%20Vincent%2C%20Glasgow))
- **Good for:** Laid-back vibes, entertainment, group gatherings

## Cocktail Bars

### Tabac
- **What:** Inventive cocktails, bartenders who'll tailor drinks to your taste
- **Where:** 10 Mitchell Lane, Glasgow G1 3NU ([map](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=10%20Mitchell%20Lane%2C%20Glasgow%20G1%203NU))
- **Good for:** Evening drinks, small groups, interesting conversation

### The Alchemist
- **What:** Theatrical cocktails and international food
- **Where:** Unit 2, George House, George Square, Glasgow G2 1EH ([map](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=George%20House%2C%20George%20Square%2C%20Glasgow%20G2%201EH))
- **Good for:** Pre-dinner drinks, spectacle, good veggie food too (see [food page](glasgow-food-veggie.html))

## Live Music Venues with Good Bars

### Mono
- **What:** Vegan cafe/bar with record store and live music
- **Where:** 12 Kings Court, King Street, Glasgow G1 5RB ([map](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=12%20Kings%20Court%2C%20King%20Street%2C%20Glasgow%20G1%205RB))
- **Good for:** Indie/alternative music, vegan food, vinyl shopping (see [food page](glasgow-food-veggie.html))

### The Hug and Pint
- **What:** Vegan food upstairs, live music downstairs
- **Where:** 171 Great Western Road, Glasgow G4 9AW ([map](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=171%20Great%20Western%20Road%2C%20Glasgow%20G4%209AW))
- **Good for:** Evening out combining food + music (see [food page](glasgow-food-veggie.html))

## Quirky/Unique Bars

### Tiki Bar & Kitsch Inn
- **What:** Tropical-themed bar with massive rum selection and cocktails in tiki mugs
- **Where:** 214 Bath Street, Glasgow G2 4HW ([map](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=214%20Bath%20Street%2C%20Glasgow%20G2%204HW))
- **Good for:** Fun group outings, rum lovers, Instagram photos

### Flight Club
- **What:** Social darts venue with vintage fairground decor, bottomless pizza, and cocktails
- **Where:** 32 North Frederick Street, Glasgow G1 2BS ([map](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=32%20North%20Frederick%20Street%2C%20Glasgow%20G1%202BS))
- **Good for:** Groups, team bonding, something different

## Whisky Bars

### The Pot Still
- **What:** Over 700 whiskies, a Glasgow institution since the 1880s
- **Where:** 154 Hope Street, Glasgow G2 2TH ([map](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=154%20Hope%20Street%2C%20Glasgow%20G2%202TH))
- **Good for:** Whisky lovers, real ale, traditional pub atmosphere

## Late-Night Options

Many Glasgow pubs close at midnight on weeknights and 1am on weekends.
For later options, Sauchiehall Street and Bath Street have the most
late-night venues.

## Quick Tips

- Glasgow pubs are generally very welcoming to strangers - don't be shy!
- Table service is rare - order at the bar
- Tipping at bars is not expected (but appreciated for table food service)
- "Last orders" is called 15 minutes before closing
- October weather: bring a jacket for the walk back!
